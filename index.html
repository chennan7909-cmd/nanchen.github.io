About

From financial data to visual stories

Course Assignments

A selection of assignments from the Visualization & Storytelling course.

Assignment 1 – Storyboard

Foundational data story sketch · Hand-drawn storyboard
Exploratory
A hand-sketched storyboard that defines the narrative arc, audience, and key message for a data-driven story. The focus is on pacing, scene transitions, and how to gradually introduce data without overwhelming the viewer.
  • Identified audience, purpose, and central question.
  • Sequenced scenes to guide attention and emotional tone.
  • Mapped where each visualization and annotation would appear.
Storyboard Narrative design Sketch-first

Assignment 2 – Interactive Redesign

From static map to interactive experience
Interactive
A redesign of an existing static visualization into an interactive experience, shifting from a purely rhetorical graphic to an exploratory interface where users can filter, zoom, and discover patterns on their own.
  • Reorganized layout to support exploration instead of a single take-away.
  • Used interaction (hover, select, filter) to reveal detail on demand.
  • Applied course principles on hierarchy, colour, and annotation.
Interaction Map redesign Exploratory data story

Assignment 3 – Infographic

Persuasive infographic · Single-page narrative
Infographic
A vertical infographic that combines charts, icons, and concise text to explain a topical issue in a clear, persuasive way. The design emphasizes rhythm, negative space, and consistent visual language.
  • Designed a poster-like layout with clear visual hierarchy.
  • Used a constrained colour and type system for coherence.
  • Balanced data density with readability for a general audience.
Poster layout Persuasion Visual hierarchy

Assignment 4 – The Algorithm's Grasp

Group project · Youth Employment & Affordability in the GTA
Vertical video
A visually dynamic, emotionally resonant vertical video concept (TikTok / Reels / Shorts) exploring how AI shapes youth employment and the affordability crisis in the Greater Toronto Area. The story interweaves official statistics with the personal narratives of two young protagonists, making a complex issue accessible to a youth-focused audience.
  • Illustrative characters (Mark & Sarah) for relatability and continuity.
  • Motion graphics with animated StatsCan charts and fast-paced text overlays.
  • Map animations contrasting downtown Toronto with outer GTA regions.
  • Sound design with modern music and subtle effects for key data moments.
AI & labour markets Youth audience Narrative + data
My contribution: I introduced the analytical lens of differentiated AI impact on the job market, showing that automation risk is not the same for all sectors. I helped categorise jobs into risk levels (routine / low-skill vs. higher-cognitive roles), giving the project a clearer socioeconomic structure and stronger analytical backbone.
